One More Radical Idea: "Knowledge Depth"

Instead of showing everything immediately, introduce a depth slider at the top of the reading experience.

Knowledge Depth

🌱 Beginner ───────●──────── Scholar

As the user moves the slider:

🌱 Beginner: Shows only the Arabic text, translation, simple explanation, and one practical takeaway.
📘 Intermediate: Reveals vocabulary, historical context, narrator biography, and related Quran verses.
🎓 Advanced: Adds chains of narration, multiple scholarly commentaries, linguistic analysis, and cross-references.

The same KnowledgeNode is rendered differently depending on the user's chosen depth. This aligns perfectly with our backend vision: the data model doesn't change—only the projection does.

My Overall Assessment

If I were scoring the current prototype against the long-term vision:

Category	Score	Notes
Visual Design	7/10	Solid foundation, but it needs a more distinctive identity.
Reading Experience	6/10	Clear, but still feels like a digital document.
Learning Experience	5/10	Lacks guided discovery and adaptive progression.
Knowledge Exploration	2/10	The relationships between concepts are not yet visible.
Innovation	4/10	Conventional educational layout rather than a new paradigm.
Scalability to Quran/Hadith/Tafsir	9/10	The underlying idea can scale well if built around KnowledgeNode.

Target Vision: 9.5–10/10.

The shift that gets us there is not primarily visual—it's conceptual. Every screen, API, and component should answer the question:

"How can this help the learner discover more knowledge from this exact piece of text?"

Once that becomes the guiding principle, the UI naturally evolves from a document reader into a Knowledge Explorer, which is fully aligned with the architecture where Knowledge is the core domain and every other feature is simply a specialized view over the same interconnected graph.
