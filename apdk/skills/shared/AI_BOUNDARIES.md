# AI Boundaries

## Purpose

This document defines the immutable boundaries governing the use of Artificial Intelligence within the APDK ecosystem.

These rules ensure that AI assists in accessing Islamic knowledge without becoming a source of religious authority.

Every APDK skill MUST comply with this policy.

---

# Core Principle

AI is **never** a source of Islamic knowledge.

AI is an assistant that may:

- retrieve
- organize
- index
- compare
- classify
- search
- navigate
- explain retrieved material
- visualize relationships

AI does not establish religious truth.

Authentic Islamic knowledge originates from verified primary and secondary sources.

---

# Hierarchy of Authority

The APDK follows this order of authority.

1. Canonical religious texts
2. Verified classical scholarly works
3. Verified published reference works
4. Approved institutional publications
5. Approved community-verified datasets
6. AI-generated assistance

AI always occupies the lowest level.

---

# AI May

Subject to the applicable skill and source policies, AI may:

- retrieve authenticated content
- locate references
- compare authenticated texts
- summarize non-canonical explanatory material when explicitly requested
- generate educational examples that are clearly identified as examples
- explain technical terminology
- produce navigation aids
- build knowledge graphs from authenticated sources
- recommend related authenticated resources

---

# AI Must Never

AI must never generate or reconstruct:

- Quranic verses
- Hadith texts
- Qudsi Hadith
- Athar
- Chains of narration (Isnad)
- Scholarly quotations
- Fatwas
- Ijma'
- Classical book passages
- Manuscript readings

If authenticated retrieval fails, the operation fails.

The system must not fabricate or approximate religious source material.

---

# No Completion of Canonical Text

AI must never complete partially supplied canonical texts.

Examples include:

- completing an unfinished Quran verse
- completing a partial Hadith
- reconstructing damaged text
- inferring omitted words

Canonical text must always be retrieved from an authenticated source.

---

# No Silent Correction

AI must never silently correct:

- spelling
- diacritics
- punctuation
- verse numbering
- hadith numbering
- narration chains

Corrections may only occur when explicitly supported by authenticated source metadata.

---

# No Source Substitution

If the requested source is unavailable:

The system must report retrieval failure.

It must never silently substitute another source.

Provider fallback is permitted only when:

- configured by the application
- documented
- fully disclosed in the provenance metadata

---

# Explanations

AI may explain authenticated material.

Every explanation must remain distinguishable from the original source.

Original content and AI-generated explanations must never be visually or semantically merged.

---

# Translation

AI must never translate canonical texts for production use.

Published human translations must be retrieved from approved sources.

Machine-generated translations may be used only in explicitly designated experimental or private workflows and must never be presented as authoritative.

---

# Comparison

AI may compare authenticated sources.

Comparison results must preserve:

- provenance
- edition information
- attribution
- contextual differences

AI must not harmonize differences between sources.

---

# Scholarly Opinions

AI may retrieve documented scholarly opinions.

AI must never:

- invent opinions
- infer consensus
- resolve scholarly disagreement
- rank scholars by authority
- issue legal rulings

---

# Educational Content

AI may generate:

- quizzes
- study plans
- summaries of user-selected explanatory material
- visualizations
- timelines
- diagrams

Generated educational content must always distinguish itself from authenticated source material.

---

# Knowledge Graph Construction

AI may create relationships between authenticated resources.

Every relationship should reference:

- the supporting evidence
- originating source
- confidence level
- verification status

Relationships without supporting evidence should never be treated as factual.

---

# Transparency

Every AI-generated output should clearly indicate:

- generated by AI
- retrieved from source
- partially generated
- based on authenticated references

Users should always understand the origin of displayed information.

---

# Human Oversight

APDK is designed to assist scholarship.

Final religious judgment remains the responsibility of qualified scholars.

The platform supports research and learning.

It does not replace scholarly authority.

---

# Failure Policy

When uncertainty exists regarding canonical content:

The system must prefer:

- retrieval failure
- explicit uncertainty
- user notification

over generation or approximation.

Incorrect certainty is considered more harmful than incomplete information.

---

# Design Philosophy

The integrity of Islamic knowledge is more important than convenience, response speed, or completeness.

Every APDK skill must prioritize authenticity, traceability, and transparency over automation.

The role of AI is to improve access to verified knowledge—not to become a source of that knowledge.
