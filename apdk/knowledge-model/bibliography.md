# BIBLIOGRAPHIC PROVENANCE

## Purpose

This document defines how AlMinhej models authentic Islamic works and preserves their complete bibliographic provenance.

Unlike traditional digital libraries, AlMinhej treats every publication history as knowledge rather than metadata.

The objective is to preserve the exact origin of every piece of indexed knowledge.

---

# Why This Matters

Many Islamic books exist in multiple textual traditions.

Examples

- Sahih al-Bukhari
- Sahih Muslim
- Al-Muwatta
- Tafsir al-Tabari
- Musnad Ahmad

Each work may have

- multiple riwayat
- multiple manuscripts
- multiple critical editions
- multiple publishers
- multiple printings
- different page numbering
- editorial annotations
- verification notes
- textual differences

These differences are valuable scholarly knowledge.

They must never be flattened into one anonymous digital text.

# Editorial Differences

Editorial differences are first-class knowledge.

Examples

- punctuation
- paragraph boundaries
- footnotes
- numbering
- textual variants
- omitted text
- additional commentary
- verification remarks

These are modeled separately from the original text.

---

# Textual Variants

Variants must never overwrite each other.

Instead they are recorded as independent observations.

Example

Variant

- location
- original wording
- alternative wording
- supporting source
- editor
- confidence
- notes

This enables scholarly comparison.

# Design Rules

Rule 1

Nothing is anonymous.

---

Rule 2

Every page has provenance.

---

Rule 3

Every Knowledge Fragment inherits provenance.

---

Rule 4

Editorial work never replaces original sources.

---

Rule 5

Variants are additive.

Never destructive.

---

Rule 6

The same Work may produce many digital projections.

The provenance remains identical.

# Future Extensions

The model intentionally supports future additions.

Examples

- multiple OCR versions
- manuscript images
- IIIF compatibility
- TEI/XML imports
- linked open data
- institutional catalogs

These extensions should integrate without changing the core hierarchy.

---

# Summary

The Bibliographic Provenance Model ensures that every piece of knowledge in AlMinhej remains traceable to its exact historical and scholarly origin.

Authenticity is preserved not only through the text itself, but through the complete chain of transmission, preservation, editing, publication and indexing.

This provenance is immutable and forms the foundation upon which Knowledge Engineering and Knowledge Projection are built.
